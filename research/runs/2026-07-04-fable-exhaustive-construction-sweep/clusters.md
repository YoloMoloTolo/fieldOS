# Pain Clusters — 2026-07-04 Exhaustive Construction Sweep

30 raw clusters, created AFTER collection from 66 evidence records (20A/33B/12C/1D) across 10
populated source families. Clusters are ordered by informal strength; formal weighted scores in
`cluster-scorecard.csv`. Record IDs refer to `evidence.jsonl`. Grade counts shown as A/B/C/D.

Legend per cluster: Records · Best evidence · Disconfirming · Families · Notes (buyer/workaround/
falsifier/next test). Marketing-language candidates only where evidence supplied real phrasing.

## C09 — Young/small-sub working capital wall
- Records: R035(A) R052(A) R053(B) R036(C) — 2A/1B/1C
- Best: 1-yr concrete co offered bigger GC work, refused LOC by every bank; factoring costs 10-20% of receipts; retention "can be all the profit from the job" (R052). Profitable $5-6M builder borrowing to make payroll, 2nd time in 5 years (R035).
- Disconfirming: veterans say years of cash-buildup suffering is the normal, survivable path; GCs already patch it with advances/joint checks (R053).
- Families: reddit ×2 threads + youtube (C10 adjacency). Coverage thin — needs forums/lender data.
- Buyer: sub owner (identifiable, reachable). Workaround spend: LOC interest, factoring quotes, Intuit credit at card rates, MCAs — real money already flowing. Incumbents: Billd, Mobilization Funding, factoring cos, banks.
- Falsifier: interviews show subs refuse to pay for anything beyond what banks/factors already offer, or GC advances are good enough.
- Marketing language: "No bank will touch a 1-year-old contracting business." / "Retention is the profit."
