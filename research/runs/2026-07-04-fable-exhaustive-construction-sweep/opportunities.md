# Top 10 Buildable Business Opportunity Areas — 2026-07-04 Sweep

Ranked by scorecard total × confidence, with adversarial notes. NONE of these is validated.
Every one requires interviews + commitment evidence before any build decision (Gate 1-3 in
research/demand-discovery-methodology.md). Scores from cluster-scorecard.csv.

---

## 1. Estimating capacity for small subs/fab shops (C12 — 83, conf 0.55)
- **Wedge**: take the estimating queue off the owner's plate for ONE trade (start: trades Eric knows — finish carpentry/remodel scopes), delivered as a service first (concierge), software later.
- **Buyer**: owner-operator who estimates nights/weekends; already proven willing to pay (offshore estimators, zzTakeoff $50/mo, junior hires).
- **First painful workflow**: inbound plan set → takeoff → priced proposal within the bid window.
- **Why now**: AI takeoff/pricing makes a US-quality, offshore-price service feasible; bid windows compressing (C11).
- **Why 2050**: whoever owns the estimate owns the transaction graph of construction.
- **Incumbents**: offshore freelancers/firms, zzTakeoff, Bluebeam, Togal/Handoff-class AI startups, in-house juniors.
- **Falsifier**: 10 interviews show estimating is guarded as the owner's core judgment (trust barrier) or churn-to-cheapest dominates.
- **Mom-Test questions**: (1) Walk me through the last bid you almost missed — what happened? (2) Who priced your last 5 jobs and what did that cost you? (3) Have you ever paid anyone else to estimate — what happened? (4) What did you bid last month that you shouldn't have? (5) Show me your takeoff for the last job that lost money.

## 2. Working capital rails for young subs (C09 — 79, conf 0.60)
- **Wedge**: NOT a lending product first — a draw/retention/receivables position dashboard that makes a young sub bankable (or that GCs use to advance safely, per R053's manual pattern).
- **Buyer**: sub owner 1-4 years in, hitting the GC-work ceiling; secondary buyer: the GC who already advances payroll manually.
- **First painful workflow**: taking a first large GC contract without dying between pay apps.
- **Why now**: 2026 spending slowdown + high rates = float pain peaks; banks refusing young books.
- **Why 2050**: construction payment terms are the industry's structural disease; fixing capital access for the smallest tier compounds.
- **Incumbents**: Billd, Mobilization Funding, factoring (10-20% of receipts per R052), MCAs, Intuit credit, GC manual advances/joint checks.
- **Falsifier**: subs treat expensive credit as a solved (if painful) problem and won't share books; GCs won't formalize advances.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) Tell me about the last job you almost couldn't make payroll on. (2) What did factoring/your LOC actually cost you last year? (3) What job did you turn down because of cash, and what was it worth? (4) Who was the last GC that advanced you money — how did that work? (5) Show me how you track what's earned-but-unpaid right now.

## 3. Back-office-in-a-box for 1-3 person trade businesses (C22 — 78, conf 0.45)
- **Wedge**: absorb the exact tasks named in evidence — phones, quoting admin, contracts, invoicing chase — for one trade niche, as a productized service.
- **Buyer**: owner-operator "banging my head against the wall" (R058); Eric's literal peer group — max founder advantage, max access.
- **First painful workflow**: the Monday-night admin stack after a full field day.
- **Why now**: AI agents make a $200-500/mo human+AI hybrid back office plausible for the first time; PE rollups (C28) squeeze independents who need leverage to stay independent.
- **Why 2050**: keeps the craft-owner tier alive against consolidation.
- **Incumbents**: VAs, answering services, bookkeepers, Jobber-tier software (which this segment refuses to configure).
- **Falsifier**: owners' stated exhaustion never converts to a recurring invoice; trust-critical tasks (quotes, client comms) won't be delegated.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) What did you do after dinner last night — walk me through it? (2) What have you already tried to get the office work off your plate — what happened? (3) What client did you lose because paperwork lagged? (4) What do you pay your bookkeeper/VA today? (5) If you closed the business like you're considering, what's the #1 thing that drove it?

## 4. Slow-pay defense stack for subs (C02+C01 — 75/61, conf 0.70)
- **Wedge**: earned-but-unpaid visibility (pay apps, retainage, notice deadlines) with state-specific escalation playbooks — the "collections copilot" a sub office manager runs weekly.
- **Buyer**: sub owner/office manager on commercial work.
- **First painful workflow**: the 60-day-and-counting receivable with a pay-when-paid clause on top of it.
- **Why now**: downturn = payment stretch; Levelset's absorption into Procore leaves the non-Procore small-sub tier ambiguously served.
- **Why 2050**: payment-chain trust is the industry's core dysfunction (US and CN evidence agree).
- **Incumbents**: Levelset/Procore, SunRay, attorneys, factoring, doing nothing.
- **Falsifier**: actual write-off rates among interviewed subs are <1-2% of revenue and they consider chasing "part of the job"; or quality-dispute entanglement (C20) means tooling can't move outcomes.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) Pull up your AR aging — walk me through the oldest three. (2) What did you write off last year? (3) Tell me about the last lien/notice you filed — what triggered it? (4) Who chases money in your shop and how many hours a week? (5) What did the last attorney letter cost and did it work?

## 5. Substantial-completion evidence & punch defense for finish subs (C08 — 70, conf 0.50)
- **Wedge**: turn the existing photo-CYA behavior into damage-attribution leverage: timestamped completion evidence per area, tied to who-was-on-site-after.
- **Buyer**: drywall/paint/flooring/trim sub owner (Eric's trade — credibility + test bed in-house).
- **First painful workflow**: the 4th punch list on the same unit, arguing whose scratch that is.
- **Why now**: phones+AI make area-level completion evidence nearly free; margins too thin to eat "hundreds of punch hours."
- **Why 2050**: quality/handoff evidence is the substrate for industrialized construction.
- **Incumbents**: CompanyCam (strong!), photos-in-camera-roll, hired punch walkers, Procore punch module (GC-owned, adversarial).
- **Falsifier**: GCs refuse sub-generated evidence in disputes; or CompanyCam already IS this and subs just don't use it (behavior, not capability, is the gap).
- **Mom-Test**: (1) On your last job, how many punch hours did you eat and what were they worth? (2) Show me the last damage-by-others fight — how did it resolve? (3) What do you photograph today and where does it go? (4) Ever billed a GC for another trade's damage — what happened? (5) What did your last hired punch-walk cost?

## 6. Owner-side bid realism & CO exposure check (C07 — 62, conf 0.55)
- **Wedge**: pre-signing "allowance adequacy + CO exposure" audit of a construction contract/bid, sold per-project.
- **Buyer**: homeowner/small CRE owner about to sign a 6-7 figure contract. One-shot buyer — likely a services/affiliate business, not SaaS.
- **Why now**: builder himself stated the underbid-then-recover playbook on the record (R038); CN evidence shows it's structural.
- **Incumbents**: owner's reps (commercial), inspectors, attorneys, r/Homebuilding.
- **Falsifier**: owners choose the low bid anyway (revealed preference); willingness-to-pay dies above ~$500.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) On your build, which line items blew past the allowance and by how much? (2) What did you check before signing? (3) Who did you pay for advice pre-contract? (4) At what point did you first feel the price was engineered? (5) What would you have paid, the day before signing, to know?

## 7. Draw-schedule orchestration for renovation loans & investor rehabs (C16 — 59, conf 0.50)
- **Wedge**: milestone/draw enforcement with photo evidence for 203k/HomeStyle/hard-money rehabs — protects owner AND keeps contractor cash-flowing on time.
- **Buyer**: first-3-deal investors and reno-loan lenders/HUD consultants (the recurring party!). Lender-side distribution dodges the novice-churn problem.
- **Falsifier**: lenders' existing inspection process is deemed adequate; contractors reject another oversight layer.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) Walk me through your last draw fight. (2) How far ahead of completed work did you pay, and why? (3) What did the stalled project cost per month? (4) [lender] What % of reno loans stall mid-draw and what does each cost you? (5) Who inspected the last draw and what did it cost?

## 8. PM↔vendor instant-pay work orders (C14 — 56, conf 0.50)
- **Wedge**: flip R040's refusal — a work-order rail where the vendor is paid on verified completion, funded by PMs who are losing vendor access.
- **Buyer**: mid-size PM firms bleeding vendors. CAUTION: the buyer is the misbehaving payer; their float economics fight the product.
- **Falsifier**: PMs won't fund escrow/instant-pay at any price that vendors would accept.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) Which trades refuse your work now and since when? (2) What's your actual median vendor-payment time — show me. (3) What did the last emergency cost because your regular vendor wouldn't come? (4) [vendor] What terms would make you take PM work again? (5) Who approves faster payment and what would they need to see?

## 9. CO pricing benchmark for GCs/owners (C05 — 57, conf 0.45)
- **Wedge**: neutral cost benchmark for common change-order scopes (the "should this grade beam CO be $15k or $40k?" answer).
- **Buyer**: GC PM on commercial; owner's reps.
- **Falsifier**: data moat unbuildable at useful granularity; relationships beat benchmarks in negotiation.
- **Mom-Test**: (1) Show me the last CO you thought was inflated — how did you check? (2) What rate caps live in your subcontracts and who enforces them? (3) When did an owner reject a CO you passed through — what happened? (4) What would you have paid for a defensible number that day? (5) How many COs per job hit real friction?
