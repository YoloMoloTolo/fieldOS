Most GC subcontracts I have seen have a standard "pay when paid" clause. In other words, you don't get paid until the GC gets his money from the Owner. If you are doing commercial, you need to be prepared to not see a dime for 60 days from the day you start. Can this be changed? Sure but the GC has to agree and IT HAS TO BE IN WRITING.
Without knowing what State you are in, Florida has a very interesting Notice to Owner statute. You, as a sub, have to notify the property owner, lender, and anyone else designated, that you are working on the job and the Owner has an obligation to make sure you get paid (eventually). If you don't file a Notice to Owner, you can't file a lien. Can't speak for other States.
Looking at your pictures, I assume you did some kind of a roof coating. I was involved with one a year or so ago. Thiogs I learned. Just like painting, the subsurface has to be acceptable. I would argue that the existing roof needed a lot of work prior to coating. You see lots of rolls, bulges, etc. Is it from water under the existing membrane? There is what appears to be a condensate line on the roof. OK, did you coat under the line? I don't see (and this may be just the photo) any type of blocking/stand/etc under the line. It's not supposed to be directly on the roof. Can't really tell what was done to the parapets, but that is usually a place where I see roof issues.
Who specified the product you used? Did a manufacturer's rep look at the job before, during and after? Did anyone do a milage test?
Were you and the manufacturer supposed to provide a warranty? Do you have it?
