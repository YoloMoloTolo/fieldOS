## 2026-07-04 — Treat scraped pain as discovery input, not demand validation

Decision: Use a staged demand-discovery method: scrape online sources to identify and rank pain
clusters, then test the highest-grade clusters with interviews, marketing pages, waitlists, manual
concierge offers, and paid preorders before deciding what software to build.

Rationale: Online complaints are biased, noisy, and cheap to produce. They are useful for finding
language, roles, workflows, and trigger events, but they do not prove willingness to pay. Paid
commitment, calendar time, data access, manual workaround spend, and repeated specific past events
are stronger indicators of demand.

Reversing condition: A simpler path consistently produces non-affiliated paid commitments faster
than the staged method without increasing false positives, legal/platform risk, or evidence-quality
problems.

## 2026-07-04 — Adopt an agentic market research operating system

Decision: Upgrade the Fable/Agent-Reach research workflow from a simple scrape-and-cluster process
to a formal market research operating system with source-family triangulation, evidence hierarchy,
cluster scorecards, adversarial review, acquisition experiments, and commitment gates.

Rationale: The older workflow could overfit to loud online complaints, AI-summarized consensus,
platform-specific memes, vendor SEO, and weak waitlist signals. A stricter operating system makes
Fable useful for breadth while reserving validation for buyer behavior, interviews, manual offers,
and paid commitments.

Reversing condition: The added structure slows learning without improving the ratio of qualified
buyer conversations, paid commitments, or killed false-positive opportunities.

## 2026-07-04 — Make exhaustive live-source evidence the Fable default

Decision: Fable and Agent-Reach research prompts must not impose artificial search caps. Numeric
record counts are minimum floors, not ceilings. Live scraped internet evidence with URLs and short
excerpts outranks prior theses, founder intuition, model priors, synthetic personas, agent
summaries, and agent opinions for discovery purposes.

Rationale: The goal is to let the market evidence surprise us. Artificial caps and thesis-weighted
synthesis would recreate confirmation bias at larger scale. Live source evidence is the best
available input for discovering what people are actually saying and doing online.

Reversing condition: Exhaustive search materially reduces evidence quality, creates unacceptable
platform/legal/account risk, or delays direct buyer conversations and paid commitment tests without
changing the top cluster rankings.

## 2026-07-04 — Make agent startup files enforce the research doctrine

Decision: Add a single research startup entrypoint and require agent instruction files to load the
Agent-Reach/Fable operating system before any internet research or synthesis pass.

Rationale: The repo had the correct methodology in specialized research docs, but the top-level
agent entrypoints did not force new agents to read them. That creates drift: a future Fable,
Claude, Codex, or chat session could use stale small-run limits, over-trust old theses, or treat
scraped complaints as validation.

Reversing condition: A simpler instruction layout consistently produces better research artifacts
with fewer agent errors, or the startup protocol becomes too heavy and delays direct buyer
interviews without improving evidence quality.

## 2026-07-04 — Use the repo as the cross-agent collaboration surface

Decision: Coordinate Fable, Claude, Codex, and Agent-Reach through the `fieldos-brain` repository:
agents must read shared startup docs, write research runs under `research/runs/`, and preserve
decisions, interviews, critique, and hypothesis state as files.

Rationale: The agents do not need direct awareness of each other if the repo acts as durable
shared memory. This reduces context loss, lets Claude/Fable collect evidence while Codex audits and
commits, and keeps the methodology visible to every agent surface.

better traceability, fewer stale artifacts, and lower operator burden than repo-mediated handoff.
